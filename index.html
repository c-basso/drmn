How Sound Masking Helps You Fall Asleep

Sleep research on noise masking points to a simple mechanism: your brain reacts to changes in sound more than to sound itself. Steady audio helps in three ways:

  • Masks disruptive noise - Traffic, neighbors, snoring, and sudden sounds blend into the background instead of standing out
  • Signals your brain to rest - The same track every night becomes a cue that it's time to wind down
  • Reduces nighttime awakenings - A constant sound floor makes you less likely to be startled awake by one-off noises

App Store reviewers most often mention falling asleep faster when background noise was the thing keeping them up. Results vary by person, so preview a few tracks and give one a full week before judging it.

What is the best white noise app for iPhone?

A good white noise app needs four things: gapless looping, background playback with the screen locked, a fade-out timer, and offline support. DRMN does all four and adds a sound mixer, so you can layer white noise with rain or fan sounds. Learn more

Can a phone app replace a sleep sound machine?

Yes. A sound machine app plays the same steady masking audio as a bedside device, adds mixing and fade timers most hardware lacks, travels with you, and skips the $50–$100 price tag. Learn more

What are the best sounds for sleep?

Steady, unchanging sounds work best: white, pink, or brown noise, rain, fan hum, and ocean waves. They mask the sudden noises — doors, traffic, snoring — that actually wake you. The right one is personal, so test each for a full night. Learn more

How do sleep sounds help you fall asleep faster?

Steady sound raises the threshold a noise needs to cross to alert your brain, and a consistent bedtime sound becomes a conditioning cue — after about a week, your brain links it to falling asleep. Learn more

Why do rain sounds help you sleep?

Rain is natural broadband noise close to pink noise: it masks disruptions without sounding artificial, and most people find it calming. Layer it with brown noise to cover low-frequency sounds too. Learn more

Can sleep sounds turn off automatically with a timer?

Yes — DRMN’s sleep timer runs from a few minutes up to 10 hours and ends with a gradual fade-out, so the stop never startles you awake the way a hard cutoff can. Learn more

Is white noise safe for babies?

Yes, when kept at or below roughly 50 decibels with the phone at least 7 feet (2 meters) from the crib, per pediatric guidance. Use a timer rather than loud all-night playback, and never put the device in the crib. Learn more

How do I block out snoring at night?

Snoring is low-frequency, so high-pitched white noise misses it. Use brown noise or fan sound — or rain layered over brown noise — set just above the snoring volume, with a long timer. Learn more

Can I get a fan sound for sleep without running a fan?

Yes. DRMN plays a gapless fan hum with no cold air, no dust, and no fan running all night. Mix it with white noise and set a fade timer. Learn more

What sounds are best for deep sleep?

Lower-frequency sounds — brown noise, pink noise, ocean waves — mask disruptions without harsh high frequencies, which makes them the usual picks for deep sleep support. Keep the volume at conversation level or below. Learn more

Yes. DRMN is free to download on the App Store. The free tier includes a sleep timer, offline playback, sound mixing, and core sleep sounds. Premium unlocks longer sessions and additional tracks — no subscription required to try the basics.

Does DRMN work offline?

Yes. After you download tracks, DRMN plays without internet. Battery optimization supports up to 10 hours of uninterrupted playback — enough for travel or all-night use.
