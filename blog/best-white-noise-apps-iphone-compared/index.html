Best White Noise Apps for iPhone Compared (2026)

Searching for the best white noise app for iPhone returns dozens of options—from minimalist fan loops to full wellness platforms. This comparison covers five widely used apps in 2026, with an emphasis on sleep masking, not meditation catalogs.

DRMN is included because it is the app behind this site; comparisons use publicly listed App Store features and typical U.S. pricing. Ratings and offers change—verify in the App Store before you subscribe.

DRMN — portable sound machine

DRMN targets users who want a bedside sound machine in app form: white, brown, and dark noise; rain, ocean, fire; baby sleep sounds; mixing; sleep timer with fade; offline playback optimized for overnight battery use.

  • Strengths: Free mixing and offline; 49.2 MB size; iOS 17.1+; 4.9/5 App Store rating (10 reviews).
  • Trade-offs: Smaller catalog than BetterSleep; no guided meditation courses or celebrity sleep stories.
  • Price: Free download; optional lifetime unlock ~$19.99.

BetterSleep — catalog breadth

BetterSleep (Relax Melodies) remains a default recommendation for people who want hundreds of sounds and presets. Premium unlocks the full library, meditations, and advanced mixes.

  • Strengths: Massive content library; established brand; sleep meditations included.
  • Trade-offs: Subscription for full access; can feel complex for simple masking needs.

Calm — wellness platform

Calm is often listed among sleep apps, but its core value is guided mindfulness. Ambient sounds exist, yet most users subscribe for meditations, classes, and sleep stories.

  • Strengths: Polished guided content; 4.8/5 with 1M+ ratings.
  • Trade-offs: Expensive if you only need white noise; less emphasis on sound-machine tooling.

White Noise Lite — minimal option

TMSOFT's White Noise Lite is a long-standing pick for basic loops—fan, rain, static—at very low cost. Good for trying masking before committing to a larger app.

  • Strengths: Simple UI; cheap premium unlock.
  • Trade-offs: Ads in free version; limited mixing and customization.

myNoise — precision tuning

myNoise appeals to users who want slider-level control over frequency bands—closer to a lab tool than a bedtime consumer app. Excellent for focus and tinnitus masking experiments.

  • Strengths: Deep customization; strong web version.
  • Trade-offs: Steeper learning curve; less "one-tap bedtime" than DRMN or White Noise Lite.

How to choose

  1. Noise masking only? Start with DRMN or White Noise Lite.
  2. Want hundreds of presets? BetterSleep.
  3. Want guided wind-down? Calm.
  4. Want frequency sliders? myNoise.
  5. Travel + offline + mixing on free tier? DRMN.

Volume and safety reminder

Regardless of app, keep bedside audio around 40–50 decibels—soft enough that normal conversation is still audible over it. Pediatric guidelines are stricter for nursery use.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free white noise app for iPhone in 2026?

For sleep masking without a subscription, DRMN and White Noise Lite are strong options. DRMN includes sound mixing, offline playback, and a one-hour sleep timer in the free tier. White Noise Lite offers basic loops with ads unless you upgrade.

Which white noise app has the best sound mixing?

DRMN and BetterSleep both support multi-layer mixing. DRMN includes mixing in its free tier. BetterSleep offers more presets but gates advanced features behind premium.

Is Calm good for white noise only?

Calm includes some ambient sounds, but it is primarily a meditation and sleep story app with a ~$69.99/year subscription. Dedicated sound-machine apps like DRMN or myNoise are usually better for masking-only use cases.

What app do sleep researchers recommend?

Researchers study sound masking principles rather than endorsing specific brands. Clinical settings often use dedicated white noise machines at 40–50 dB. Any app that plays steady, moderate-volume audio can serve a similar function.

Which app works best offline overnight?

DRMN and myNoise are designed for downloaded tracks and long sessions. DRMN emphasizes battery optimization for all-night iPhone playback without Wi‑Fi.
