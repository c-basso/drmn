Best white noise apps for iPhone are mobile apps that play steady ambient audio (white, pink, or brown noise, rain, fan hum, and similar loops) to mask sudden bedroom sounds and support sleep onset. They differ from meditation platforms: the goal is background sound you stop noticing rather than guided voice content.

What to look for in a white noise app

Before comparing brands, decide which features matter for your bedroom:

  • Seamless loops: short repeats your brain learns to track can become a distraction.
  • Offline downloads: essential for travel, weak Wi-Fi, or airplane mode overnight.
  • Mixing: layering rain under brown noise often masks better than a single preset.
  • Sleep timer with fade: helpful if you only need audio at sleep onset.
  • Background playback: the app should keep playing when the screen locks.

White Noise Lite: minimal option

TMSOFT's White Noise Lite is a long-standing pick for basic loops (fan, rain, static) at very low cost. Good for trying masking before committing to a larger app.

myNoise: precision tuning

myNoise appeals to users who want slider-level control over frequency bands. It feels closer to a calibration tool than a one-tap bedtime app. Useful for focus and tinnitus masking experiments.

  • Strengths: Deep customization; strong web version.
  • Trade-offs: Steeper learning curve; less instant bedtime setup than DRMN or White Noise Lite.

How to choose

  1. Noise masking only? Start with DRMN or White Noise Lite.
  2. Want hundreds of presets? BetterSleep.
  3. Want guided wind-down? Calm.
  4. Want frequency sliders? myNoise.
  5. Travel + offline + mixing on free tier? DRMN.

Test one app for a full week at the same measured volume before switching. Loudness matters more than brand name.

Volume and iPhone setup tips

Regardless of app, keep bedside audio around 40 to 50 decibels, soft enough that normal conversation is still audible over it. Place the phone or speaker several feet from your pillow, not on the mattress next to your ear.

On iPhone, confirm background audio works with your Focus or Do Not Disturb settings. Download tracks before travel so playback does not depend on streaming. Pediatric guidelines are stricter for nursery use: lower volume, more distance from the crib, and ask your pediatrician when unsure.

Medical disclaimer: Sleep and focus audio supports healthy habits; it is not a substitute for clinical care. If poor sleep persists for weeks, you snore loudly, or you feel exhausted despite long time in bed, talk to a healthcare provider.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free white noise app for iPhone in 2026?

For sleep masking without a subscription, DRMN and White Noise Lite are strong options. DRMN includes sound mixing, offline playback, and a one-hour sleep timer in the free tier. White Noise Lite offers basic loops with ads unless you upgrade.

Which white noise app has the best sound mixing?

DRMN and BetterSleep both support multi-layer mixing. DRMN includes mixing in its free tier. BetterSleep offers more presets but gates advanced features behind premium.

Is Calm good for white noise only?

Calm includes some ambient sounds, but it is primarily a meditation and sleep story app with a typical annual subscription near $70 in the U.S. Dedicated sound-machine apps like DRMN or myNoise are usually better for masking-only use cases.

What app do sleep researchers recommend?

Researchers study sound masking principles rather than endorsing specific brands. Clinical settings often use dedicated white noise machines at 40 to 50 dB. Any app that plays steady, moderate-volume audio can serve a similar function.

Which app works best offline overnight?

DRMN and myNoise are designed for downloaded tracks and long sessions. DRMN emphasizes battery optimization for all-night iPhone playback without Wi-Fi.
