BetterSleep Alternative for iPhone (2026)

By Vladimir Ivakhnenko

A BetterSleep alternative for iPhone is a sleep audio app that covers your core bedtime needs (steady masking loops, mixing, offline playback, sleep timer) without the subscription weight of a large content platform. BetterSleep, formerly Relax Melodies, is built around hundreds of presets and guided programs. Alternatives like DRMN target sound-machine use cases with a leaner free tier.

If you searched for a BetterSleep alternative, you likely want lower cost, fewer paywalls, or a simpler masking workflow. This guide compares DRMN and BetterSleep for sleep-only needs. DRMN is included because it powers this site; feature notes reflect publicly listed App Store information and may change by region.

Why people look for BetterSleep alternatives

  • Subscription fatigue: BetterSleep premium is typically around $59.99 per year in the U.S. for full library access.
  • Feature gating: free users often hit limits on sounds, mixes, or session length.
  • Complexity: large catalogs and upsells can feel heavy if you only need steady rain or fan noise.
  • Battery use: all-night playback with many layers can drain battery on older iPhones.
  • Masking vs content: if you never open meditations, you may be paying for a platform when you need a tool.

What to prioritize in an alternative

Before switching apps, list what you actually use nightly:

  • One steady loop (rain, fan, pink noise) vs dozens of presets you never open.
  • Offline downloads for travel and weak Wi-Fi bedrooms.
  • Mixing two or three layers without a paywall.
  • Sleep timer with fade if you only need audio at sleep onset.
  • Background playback when the screen locks and Focus mode is on.

When DRMN is the better BetterSleep alternative

DRMN fits if you want a straightforward sound machine without navigating a large premium catalog:

  • You mainly need white, pink, or brown noise plus rain, ocean, or fan sounds.
  • You want to mix two or three layers without paying monthly.
  • You travel often and need reliable offline playback.
  • You use a sleep timer with fade-out and do not want audio running all night.
  • You prefer a smaller app footprint and battery-friendly overnight use.

When BetterSleep still wins

BetterSleep remains strong if you value:

  • Hundreds of pre-built mixes and frequent new content drops.
  • Integrated bedtime meditations and breathing programs.
  • A mature ecosystem you already paid for and customized over years.
  • Guided wind-down content, not only steady masking loops.

If guided programs are your main habit, staying subscribed may make more sense than forcing a sound-only app to replace them.

Migration tips: switching from BetterSleep to DRMN

  1. Identify your top two sounds in BetterSleep, for example heavy rain plus distant thunder.
  2. Recreate the mix in DRMN with rain plus brown noise at low volume.
  3. Download tracks for offline use before your first night.
  4. Set a 45 to 60 minute timer with fade if you only need help falling asleep.
  5. Keep volume moderate (40 to 50 dB). Louder is not more effective. See sound volume and sleep quality.
  6. Run one week unchanged before judging; switching nightly hides what actually helped.

Volume and iPhone setup

Regardless of app, keep bedside audio around 40 to 50 decibels, soft enough that normal conversation is still audible over it. Place the phone or speaker several feet from your pillow.

On iPhone, confirm background audio works with Focus and Do Not Disturb. Download tracks before travel so playback does not depend on streaming. Sleep audio supports habits; it does not fix overheated rooms, late caffeine, or untreated sleep disorders.

Medical disclaimer: Sleep and focus audio supports healthy habits; it is not a substitute for clinical care. If poor sleep persists for weeks, you snore loudly, or you feel exhausted despite long time in bed, talk to a healthcare provider.

Key takeaways

  • A BetterSleep alternative for iPhone makes sense when you need masking, mixing, and offline playback more than a huge content library.
  • DRMN targets sound-machine workflows with a stronger free tier; BetterSleep targets preset breadth and guided sleep programs behind subscription.
  • Prioritize seamless loops, offline downloads, and a sleep timer before comparing catalog size.
  • Recreate your favorite mix, test one volume level for a week, and verify pricing in the App Store before canceling.
  • Keep BetterSleep if guided meditations and preset variety are what you actually use nightly.
